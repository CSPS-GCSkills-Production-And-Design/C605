Question 1

Last month, you were sick; as you had no paid sick days left, you had to take a day of leave without pay (LWOP).

Will this LWOP appear as a deduction in the Entitlements and Deductions section of your CWA Pay Stub?

Correct - The LWOP will appear in the Entitlements and Deductions section of your CWA Pay Stub and will indicate the Pay Period in which the LWOP took place.

Incorrect - The LWOP will appear in the Entitlements and Deductions section of your CWA Pay Stub and will indicate the Pay Period in which the LWOP took place.

Question 2

You accepted a new position in Winnipeg, and moved from Gatineau to Winnipeg to occupy this new position in another division.

You want to ensure that the income tax deductions reflect your new place of work. Will the Phoenix Pay Stub indicate your province of work?

Correct - The Phoenix Pay stub will indicate your mailing address in the General information section, but not your province of work, which determines the income tax to be deducted from your pay.

Your province of work will be indicated on your CWA Pay Stub, in the General information section.

The Phoenix Pay stub will however, convey tax data information such as your Federal and Provincial Net Claim.

Incorrect - The Phoenix Pay stub will indicate your mailing address in the General information section, but not your province of work, which determines the income tax to be deducted from your pay.

Your province of work will be indicated on your CWA Pay Stub, in the General information section.

The Phoenix Pay stub will however, convey tax data information such as your Federal and Provincial Net Claim.

Question 3

You are considering taking a 5-month leave without pay (LWOP) for the care of your family.

You read you would have to pay both the employer’s share and your share of benefits for part of your leave. You want to estimate these extra costs before making any decision.

Will the Phoenix Pay Stub provide you the information you need on your employer’s contributions to your benefits?

Correct - The Employer’s share will appear in the Employer-Paid Benefits section of your Pay Stub. You should bear in mind some of these benefits depend on your salary (which may increase during your LWOP due to the yearly pay revision).

Incorrect - The Employer’s share will appear in the Employer-Paid Benefits section of your Pay Stub. You should bear in mind some of these benefits depend on your salary (which may increase during your LWOP due to the yearly pay revision).

Question 4

You had to take two days of leave without pay a month ago. When you look at your Phoenix pay stub, you notice under earnings a LWOP entry displaying an amount, and a basic pay line displaying a negative amount. It would seem as if your two days were deducted twice from you pay. Is this the case?

Correct! - For a period of LWOP, there will be two entries in the Earnings section. One as a basic pay line with line with a negative amount which is deducted from your pay and another as a LWOP amount that is used for Pension calculations only.

Incorrect. - For a period of LWOP, there will be two entries in the Earnings section. One as a basic pay line with line with a negative amount which is deducted from your pay and another as a LWOP amount that is used for Pension calculations only.

Question 5

You know that your annual salary does not appear on your Phoenix pay stub, but you see that your biweekly salary does. As a full-time employee, you want to calculate your annual salary, so you decide to multiply your bi-weekly salary by 26 weeks. Will you be able to determine your annual salary by doing this?

Correct! - If you are a full-time employee, to calculate your annual gross salary, you must multiple your biweekly salary by 26.088. This multiplier accounts for the uneven number of complete pay periods.

Incorrect - If you are a full-time employee, to calculate your annual gross salary, you must multiple your biweekly salary by 26.088. This multiplier accounts for the uneven number of complete pay periods.

A Few Words on Taxes…

When you were hired, you complete Personal Tax Credits Return forms (TD1’s, TP1015), so your employer could determine the amount of tax to deduct from your income.

The amounts you requested as personal exemptions for income tax would be displayed in the following fields:

  • Fed Net Claim: Federal Net Claim - Personal exemption amount for federal income tax.
  • MB Net Claim or QC Net Claim: Manitoba or Quebec Net Claim - Personal exemption amount for Manitoba or Quebec income tax. Please note the information in this field will change based on your province of work (for example, BC Net Claim if you work in BC).

If you requested for additional taxes to be deducted from your pay …

These amounts would be displayed in the following fields:

  • Fed Addl Amount: Federal Additional Amount you want to be deducted from your pay.
  • QC Addl Amount: Quebec Additional Amount you want to be deducted from your pay.

Please note additional taxes have to be of a specific amount (percentage option is not available).

What do Fed or QC Spcl Letters stand for?

Under very specific circumstances, the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) and/or Revenu Quebec may give the employer permission to reduce the amount of income tax to deduct from an employee’s pay. These Special Letters, or letters of authority, have to be requested by the employee directly.

For more information on these, please visit CRA’s and/or Revenu Quebec’s websites.

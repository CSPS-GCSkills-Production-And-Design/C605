Phoenix Pay Stub

Employer Paid Benefits Section

Did you know your employer contributes to your benefits?

What the employer pays for your different plans and coverage will be displayed in this section.

Notice that the information may be different depending on different factors such as:

  • your province of work
  • your employment status
  • the types and levels of insurance coverage.

Please note: these amounts are nor paid to you, nor deducted from your pay.

A few details on the employer paid benefits:

PSHCP: Your employer contributes to your Public Service Health Care Plan, for all Levels of coverage.

PSDCP: Your Public Service Dental Care Plan is entirely covered by your employer.

PSSA and Sup Death Benefit: Your employer contributes to both your Pension (Public Service Superannuation Act) and your Supplementary Death Benefit.

Disability: Your employer also contributes to your disability insurance (DI) plan. This section could instead display Long Term Disability if you are eligible for the Public Service Management Insurance Plan (PSMIP).

The lines for PSHCP and PSDCP will appear twice in this Section if you work in the province of Quebec, as these are taxable benefits for the Province of Quebec.

What are taxable benefits?

Taxable benefits are benefits paid by the employer which are considered as part of your income for income tax purposes.

For example, if you work and/or live in the province of Quebec, the employer paid portion of the Dental and Health Care plans are taxable benefits.

Before Tax and After Tax Deductions Sections

While Before Tax Deductions are deducted from your earnings before income tax is calculated, After Tax Deductions are deducted from your earnings after income tax is calculated.

All these deductions depend on your employment status, and the benefits you are entitled to.

Your deductions may include:

  • Dues: Union dues, and to which Union they are paid to.
  • PSSA: Public Service Superannuation Act (Pension plan).
  • PSHCP: An amount will only be displayed if you contribute to Level II or Level III of the Public Service Health Care Plan (PSHCP). The premium for the Level I plan is $0.
  • Supplementary Death Benefit: The Supplementary Death Benefit (SDB) is a form of decreasing life insurance. If you contribute to the Public Service Pension plan (for the majority of active members), you will also contribute to this benefit.
  • Disability Insurance: The disability insurance is a plan that provides income replacement when you are unable to work for long periods due to a totally disabling illness or injury. This section could instead display Long Term Disability if you are eligible for the Public Service Management Insurance Plan (PSMIP).

For more information on your benefits including contribution rates, we invite you to visit TBS’ “Benefit plans”.

What do PSSA - Group 2 - Low / PSSA - Group 2 - High mean?

Your Public Service Pension plan deduction (PSSA, which stands for Public Service Superannuation Act) is calculated at either a low or high rate.


The low rate is used when your current annual salary is below a specific threshold. Once your annual salary exceeds this amount, your deduction changes to the high rate.

Group 2 – refers to plan members who began to participate in the Public Service Pension plan (PSSA) on or after January 1, 2013.


The change from a low rate to a high rate usually happens at the same time that your maximum yearly CPP or QPP contribution has been paid.

Group 1 – refers to plan members who were participating to PSSA on or before December 31, 2012. If you belong to Group 1, the line will simply display “PSSA – Low” or “PSSA – High”.

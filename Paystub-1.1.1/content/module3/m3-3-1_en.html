Comparing Both Types of Pay Stub

Leave Without Pay (Cont’d)

As mentioned earlier in this course, if you are a part-time employee, statutory holidays will be deducted from your pay, as you are not entitled to paid statutory holidays.

You will, however, receive a 4.25% premium in lieu (replacement) of statutory holidays.

Let’s see an example on how this information would be displayed on your pay stubs.

You are a part-time PM-1 employee.

  • Your work schedule is Monday to Thursday, and you work 30 hours a week, 7.5 hours per day.
  • You just received your pay, which covers the period starting Thursday July 26, 2018 and ending Wednesday August 8, 2018.
  • After consulting your Phoenix Pay Stub, you notice 15 hours of LWOP were deducted from your pay.

  • You see 7.5 hours were deducted for a LWOP that occurred in the 26/07/18 To 08/08/18 Pay Period (PP).

  • There are also 7.5 hours deducted for a LWOP that occurred in the 28/06/18 to 11/07/18 Pay Period.

Comparing Both Types of Pay Stub

Overtime – Cont’d

The same concept will apply to payments which include Meals and Shift Premiums, as both pay stubs will confirm the breakdown by type of premium, the number of meals or shift premiums paid, the premium rate, and the amount paid.

Once again, only the CWA Pay Stub will indicate the Pay Period for which the premium is paid, as well as the breakdown by Pay Period.

First, let’s go over the meal allowance. To do so, we will take an example similar to the one displayed earlier.

You are a full-time AS-2. This time, instead of working 2 hours of overtime per day, you worked:

  • 5 hours of OT on Wednesday September 5, 2018
  • 5 hours of OT on Thursday September 13, 2018

As indicated in your collective agreement, you are entitled to the reimbursement of one $10 meal when you work 3 hours or more of OT immediately before or immediately after your scheduled hours of work.

Upon consulting your Phoenix Pay Stub, you can confirm you were paid a total of 2 meals at the rate of $10 per meal.

Now, let’s go over the shift premium. Please bear in mind this premium only applies to shift work employees.

You are a full-time GT-3. You worked a few evening shifts to replace one of your colleagues:

  • a total of 14 hours between March 22 and April 4, 2018
  • and a total of 49 hours between April 5 and 18, 2018

As indicated in your collective agreement, an employee working on shifts will receive a shift premium of $2 per hour for all hours worked, including OT hours, between 4:00 PM and 08:00 AM.

Upon consulting your Phoenix Pay Stub, you can confirm you were paid a total of 2 meals at the rate of $10 per meal.

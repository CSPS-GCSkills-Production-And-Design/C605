A few words on Pay fluctuations - cont'd

You just received your pay, and there are no more deductions for CPP or QPP, EI, QPIP (PPIP) … and your pension contributionsare now at the high rate.

Is this correct?

A few factors might have a direct incidence on your net pay, such as …

Reaching maximum contributions to CPP, QPP, EI and QPIP

There are yearly (calendar year) maximum contributions for each of theseplans.

Depending on your salary, you may eventually reach the maximumcontributions.

If you have contributed the maximum for a plan, no further deductions willbe withheld from your pay until the first payment in the next calendaryear.

Starting a new year, contributions restart

As a new year begins, income tax deductions may increase, as they willbe revised by both provincial and federal governments. Contributions to CPP, QPP, EI and QPIP will also be revised. These increases will affect yournet pay.

If you had reached the maximum deductions for CPP, QPP, EI and QPIP at some time inthe previous year, at the beginning of the new year, these deductionswill start to be deducted again and will affect your net pay.

For more information on the yearly maximum contributions, please referto the Canada Revenue Agency’s page entitled “What is deducted from your pay?”.

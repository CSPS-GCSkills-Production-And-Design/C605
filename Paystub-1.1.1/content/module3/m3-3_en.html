Comparing Both Types of Pay Stub

Leave Without Pay

As seen in the previous lessons, if you were absent on Leave without Pay (LWOP) for 5 consecutive days or less, both pay stubs will indicate the number of hours and amount deducted from your pay.

However, only the CWA Pay Stub will also indicate the Pay Period in which the LWOP actually occurred.

Let’s see an example on how this information would be displayed on your pay stubs.

You are a full-time casual PM-1 employee.

  • You were sick and could not report to work on Wednesday, July 11 and Thursday July 12, 2018.
  • Being a casual employee, you have no paid sick days, which means your days of absence will be deducted from your pay as leave without pay (LWOP).
  • You just received your pay, which covers the period starting Thursday August 9, 2018 and ending Wednesday August 22, 2018.
  • After consulting your Phoenix Pay Stub, you notice 15 hours of LWOP were deducted from your pay.

  • You see 7.5 hours were deducted for a LWOP that occurred in the 28/06/18 to 11/07/18 Pay Period (PP).
    • As your sick day of Wednesday July 11th falls into this PP, it is what is being deducted.
  • You see 7.5 hours were deducted for a LWOP that occurred in the 12/07/18 to 25/07/18 Pay Period (PP).
    • As your sick day of Thursday July 12th falls into this PP, it is what is being deducted.

Now why not just reduce the number of hours being paid instead of using separate lines of Basic (or Acting) Pay for LWOP deductions?

Well, as the Pay System is automated, by default you are automatically paid for your regular work schedule (full-time or part-time regular hours). This is displayed on the first Basic (or Acting) Pay line.

Consequently, the deduction for your hours of LWOP has to be made on different lines.

Furthermore, as you can notice on the above example, there usually is a gap between the period covered by your pay, and the pay period during which the LWOP occurred. On the above, the pay covers August 9 to 22, while the LWOP took place in July.

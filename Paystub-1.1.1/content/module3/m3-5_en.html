Comparing Both Types of Pay Stub

A few words on Pay fluctuations

You just received your pay, and it is higher than usual … are you entitled to this?

Here are a few circumstances that might affect the amount you receive!

I heard there was a “big pay” twice a year…

  • Indeed. You usually receive 2 pays per month, and monthly deductions for Union, Death Benefits, Disability Insurance, and Medical Insurance are split over these 2 pays.
  • However, typically twice a year, there are 3 pay periods in a month!
  • During those months, Union and other Insurances will only be deducted on the second and third pay, so your first monthly pay will be higher!

Note: statutory deductions (income tax, CPP or QPP, EI, QPIP) will still be deducted on these pays.

Any other good news?

  • Yes! Each year, your salary increases because of a pay revision which is part of your Collective Agreement. Each Collective Agreement has a set date for yearly pay revisions.
  • For example, for the PA group, the rate of pay increases on June 21*.

*Note: if the collective agreement has expired, the rates will be revised retroactively, once a new agreement is signed.

Collective agreements are available on the page entitled: “Collective agreements for public service”

Unrepresented and senior excluded employees’ rates of pay are available on the page entitled: “Rates of pay for unrepresented and senior excluded employees”

More good news!

  • You could be entitled to receive a salary increase on the anniversary date you were appointed at your current position. This salary increase is referred to as a Step in your Collective Agreement.
  • If you have already reached the last step for your group and level, this will not apply and you remain at that last step.
  • For example, if for example if you are a PM-1 and are already paid at Step 4, your salary would remain at that step.

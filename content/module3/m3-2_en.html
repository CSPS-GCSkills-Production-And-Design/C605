Comparing Both Types of Pay Stub

Overtime

As seen in lessons 1 and 2, if you have worked Overtime (also referred to as Extra Duty Pay), both pay stubs will indicate the type of OT, total hours worked and amount paid.

However, only the CWA Pay Stub will also indicate the Pay Period in which the OT was worked.

Let’s see an example of how this information would be displayed on your pay stubs.

You are a full-time employee, working 37.5 hours a week.

During a peak period at work, your manager asked you to work overtime (OT). You worked and submitted the following hours:

  • 2 hours on Wednesday September 5, 2018
  • 2 hours on Thursday September 6, 2018
  • 2 hours on Sunday, September 9, 2018
  • 2 hours on Thursday September 20, 2018

There is usually a gap between the time you worked OT and the time it actually appears on your pay. A number of weeks later, you consult your Phoenix Pay Stub and notice you were paid:

  • 4 hours of OT worked on your regular work days (Regular), and
  • 2 hours of OT worked on a Sunday (Second Day of Rest).

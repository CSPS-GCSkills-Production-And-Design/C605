Comparing Both Types of Pay Stub

A few words on Pay fluctuations - cont'd

You just received your pay, and there are no more deductions for CPP or QPP, EI, QPIP (PPIP) … and your pension contributions are now at the high rate.

Is this correct?

A few factors might have a direct incidence on your net pay, such as …

Reaching maximum contributions to CPP, QPP, EI and QPIP

There are yearly (calendar year) maximum contributions for each of these plans.

Depending on your salary, you may eventually reach the maximum contributions.

If you have contributed the maximum for a plan, no further deductions will be withheld from your pay until the first payment in the next calendar year.

Starting a new year, contributions restart

As a new year begins, income tax deductions may increase, as they will be revised by both provincial and federal governments. Contributions to CPP, QPP, EI and QPIP will also be revised. These increases will affect your net pay.

If you had reached the maximum deductions for CPP, QPP, EI and QPIP at some time in the previous year, at the beginning of the new year, these deductions will start to be deducted again and will affect your net pay.

For more information on the yearly maximum contributions, please refer to the Canada Revenue Agency’s page entitled “What is deducted from your pay?”.

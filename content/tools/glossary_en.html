Glossary

Note: You should always refer to your collective agreement or to the terms and conditions of employment to see if the following apply to you.

  • ADDITIONAL HOURS

    Applies to part-time employees only.

    Additional hours are hours worked that do not exceed the regular workday hours (7.5 or 8.0) or weekly hours (37.5 or 40).

  • ASSOCIATION DUES

    Union Dues paid for membership of a union.

  • BILINGUALISM BONUS

    The bilingualism bonus consists of an annual payment of $800, calculated on a monthly basis and paid on the same basis as regular pay.

    Employees occupying a position identified as bilingual can be entitled to a bilingualism bonus according to certain rules.

  • EXTRA DUTY PAY

    Overtime refers to any hours worked over and above the employee’s standard daily or weekly hours of work based on their collective agreement.

  • FIRST DAY OF REST

    This refers to Saturday for employees working from Monday to Friday. Please refer to your collective agreement to learn more.

  • LONG TERM DISABILITY INSURANCE (LTD)

    LTD insurance is part of the Public Service Management Insurance Plan (PSMIP) for unrepresented and excluded employees occupying confidential positions or executives.

  • MEAL ALLOWANCE

    An entitlement due to employees for reimbursement of meal expenses when they are required to work a minimum specified period of overtime immediately before or immediately following their regular work hours.

    Please refer to your collective agreement for more information.

  • RECOVERY

    Deduction related to unearned salary (for example, for unpaid leave) or allowance (for example bilingual bonus, education allowance, etc.).

  • BENEFITS

    https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/topics/benefit-plans/plans.html

  • LWOP

    A leave period for which you are not entitled to be paid.

  • SHIFT PREMIUM

    Applies only to employees on shift work.

    Compensation may be paid to employees on shift work.

    (Note: Reference should be made to the relevant collective agreement for the actual hours of work of the different shifts.)

  • SUPERANNUATION / PENSION (PSSA)

    Public Service Pension contributions. Term used by Federal Public Service to refer to pension.

  • STATUTORY DEDUCTIONS

    Mandatory deductions required to be deducted from your pay.

  • BASIC PAY

    Regular hours paid, before any extras (such as premiums, allowances, or OT) are added or taken off (such as LWOP) from your pay.

  • GROSS PAY

    The total amount of pay before any deductions are taken out.

  • NET PAY

    The amount of pay remaining after deductions are taken out.

  • YEAR TO DATE

    The total amount of pay and deductions from the beginning of the year through the current pay period.

  • PPIP / QPIP

    An income replacement plan that supports parent of a newborn child. This is a mandatory deduction is you work in the province of Quebec.

  • BEFORE TAX DEDUCTIONS

    These deductions are calculated before any taxes have been deducted from your gross pay.

  • AFTER TAX DEDUCTIONS

    These deductions are calculated after taxes have been deducted.

  • EMPLOYER PAID BENEFITS

    Amounts contributed by the employer on your behalf.
